As a follow up, we would like to send you a list of links to resources 
in case you want to learn more.

The first thing that I can recommend is to attend a CodeRefinery tool online workshop.
which covers he collaborative aspect of Git (GitHub and similar platforms) further,
reproducible research, open software, documentation, 
automated testing and modular code development. 
Most topics are discussed in a language-independent way.
The next edition will be held at the beginning of Autumn 2025 (exact dates still TBD),
and will be announced on the mainling lists you are already subscribed to.

If you really cannot wait, all the lessons of CodeRefinery are also available on youtube at
https://www.youtube.com/@coderefinery.

If you want to practice more your Python, there are several options.
If you like mathematics and numeric problems, https://projecteuler.net/ might suit your taste.



Other things to mention:
For those of you who are more interested in data science, the R and SQL courses
in the same style are also available at the carpentries website (https://carpentries.org/lessons/).
Unfortunately I am not aware of anyone teaching them in the near future in Germany,
but the material is typically considered suitable for self-studying,
especially if you have had some programming experience before.
